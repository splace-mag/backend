KV107, Monika Riedmüller, Fashion collection 2013/14, Photo: Elfie Semotan

KV107

Monika Riedmüller

For her final collection “KV 107” Monika Riedmüller was inspired by tone colours: With a specially designed code she translated compositions into structured colour patterns, creating a system of colour combinations she wouldn’t necessarily have picked herself. This self-imposed dictatorship leads to a shift of viewing habits. In order to find a connection between the rigid colour patterns and the moving music, she picked classical and sporty shapes that come together in these unique designs.

bark, Miriam Kohlhofer, Abschlußkollektion / Mode, Stoffe für Konfektionsteile aus Baumwolle, Seide und Wolle Strick aus Merino und Alpaka, 2013 – 2014, Foto: Elfie Semotan

bark

Miriam Kohlhofer

Miriam Kohlhofer final collection is called “bark” and deals with tree bark, shells and layers. She thereby examines the shell of space as a habitat for the body. Structures and shapes are used for the inspiration for silhouettes and combinations of materials.

Daily Way Home, Katharina Diezl, Final Collection 2013/14 – variouse textiles; collection consists of 12 outfits / pieces, Photo: Elfie Semotan

Daily Way Home

Katharina Diezl

The aesthetics of decay – inspired by old Venetian buildings and their strong colours shining in contrast to the water – are the focus of the work “Daily Way Home”. Looking at this simultaneity of construction and abrasion Katharina Diezl identified analogies to local galleries and pavilions. For her collection she focused on a very accurate translation of the three-dimensional facades into her knitted material. The pattern of the screen printed fabric was reduced to the bare contrast of colours, clear cuts proved to be the best canvas for the barely noticed “street art”.

I'll put you through, Flavia Barandun, 2014/15, painting and graphics, acrylic & fineliner pen on paper, Photo: Flavia Barandun

I'll put you through

Barandun Flavia

In her work Flavia Barandun is concerned about the context of single individuals, what they face in everyday life, their memories and dreams. From these ordinary memories and dreams Barandun unfolds an illusory world on paper, which is yet closely linked to the actual world of the artist herself.

Titel durch Klicken hinzufügen – Folie 1, Doris Bujatti, 2013, textile object, quilt from cotton, 205 x 145 cm, Photo: Christoph Panzer

Titel durch Klicken hinzufügen - Folie 1

Dori Bujatti

The time-consuming tradition of quilting – sewing together small pieces of fabric – stands for sustainability and decor. Communication, discussion and the exchange of knowledge is in the centre of this collaborative handcraft of women. PowerPoint on the other hand is a tool used to illustrate ideas and present information as well as relations in a very rational manner.

In her project Doris Bujatti uses PowerPoint slides to represent emotional connections by attaching one to the other. The quilting of the slides causes an emotionalisation of relations. The quilt thereby turns the rational PowerPoint slides into a tactile and emotional subject.

BullShut App, Marta Pérez Campos, 2015, mobile application / interactive installation, Photo: Marta Pérez Campos

BULLSHUT APP

Marta Pérez Campos

BullShut App is a mobile application helping to avoid awkward moments in various social situations. BullShut App establishes a space for communication between two individuals for a short time. By entering a specific topic other interested users can be found and connected to engage in an immediate conversation.

respekt, Stepana Cihlova, 2015, photographs 90 x 60 cm, Photo: Stepana Cihlova

respekt

Stepana Cihlova

With her photo series “respekt” Stepana Cihlova refers to the issue of mass production. Single individuals usually experience better treatment than within a mass. Based on her portraits of cows, she reveals that on closer inspection every single one of them has specific features, a unique personality, mood and look. These characteristics may lead to a change of care and attitude.

widedarkspace, Margit Maria Anna Erber, 2015, 35mm film, b/w video (FullHD 1920 x 1080 px), audio, 3:00 min

widedarkspace

Margit Maria Anna Erber

“widedarkspace” is a 35mm animation film made of panoramic photographs. The digitised and moving images show various places in Helsinki marked with coordinates to allow a spatial integration into the urban fabric. By using field recordings an authentic sound collage of the city was created.

Die Vergangenheit der Gegenwart des Zukünftigen, Susanne Evelyn Erlach, 2014, photography, pinhole camera, 70 x 25 cm, Photo: Susanne Evelyn Erlach

Die Vergangenheit der Gegenwart des Zukünftigen

Susanne Evelyn Erlach

With the work “Die Vergangenheit der Gegenwart des Zukünftigen” Susanne Erlach combines two analog photographs into a joint image. Both photographs were taken at different times, but from the same place. Both times the pedestrians who move in opposite directions were accompanied part of their way. By merging the shots the two perspectives come together – carried along in two opposite directions.

melting point, Melanie Greußing, photography 2015, 25 x 28 cm, Photo: Melanie Greußing

melting point

Melanie Greußing

Cold and darkness prevail the winter in the North. What relevance do public sites as places for encounter have, when light is rare and the temperature prevents lingering? Where and how can communication take place under such circumstances? With her work “Melting point” Melanie Greußig is seeking answers to these questions on one of many ice rinks. Here sport becomes public interaction and the body replaces language as means of expression.

RIP, Felix Huber, multichannel video indstallation 2:30 (loop), Still: Felix Huber

RIP

Huber Felix

“RIP” is a multi-channel video installation of various camera shots moving over latent cemeteries on the verge of busy places in Linz. These field of contrasting context is made visible in the interplay of sound and image.

Mimik der Farben (violet), Petra Kettl, 2015, photographic study, Photo: Petra Kettl

violett

Petra Kettl

In “Mimik der Farben” Petra Kettl examines the subjective sensation, preference, dislike or indifference to a colour. On the basis of a photographic study she was trying to identify a corresponding reflex in the faces of the viewers. Visible colours of the spectrum have been selected to test and record the subjective response of three persons photographically thereby composing a new graphical arrangement.

Die Fülle der Leere - Die Leere der Fülle., Carina Kinzel, sound installation – foam box with integrated mp3-player, digitally combined audio files of background noises of a shopping mall, headphones, Photo: Carina Kinzel

Die Fülle der Leere - Die Leere der Fülle.

Carina Kinzel

The installation “Die Fülle der Leere - Die Leere der Fülle.” is an exploration of emptiness, wealth and covers in five scenes that question the aspect of consumption of our times and the nature of man per se. Which emptiness are we so eager to fill? What wealth can eliminate the void? Is material wealth in the end nothing but emptiness? What is emptiness? What is wealth? And what is left once all covers are unveiled?

Transient References, Merve Köklü, 30 x 30 x 5 cm, box and projection screen, Photo: Merve Köklü

Transient References

Merve Köklü

Merve Köklü’s project was inspired by colour definitions named after substances found in nature. Colours and shapes of these objects change over time. “Transient References” makes these transitions visible and deals with the mortality and ephemeral nature of life. It reveals the arbitrariness of earthly references and shows how they can change within the context of time.

in between, Claudia N. Lehmann / Esthaem, 2014/15, photography 50 x 70 cm, Photo: Claudia N. Lehmann / Esthaem

in between

Claudia N Lehmann/Esthaem

The joint project “in between” by Claudia Lehmann and Manuel Mayr is about the subjective perception of colours. The complementary colours have been divided between the two artists, enhancing the contrast between the complementary pairs. The collective work is an intimate exploration of the emotional aspects of colours and their visualisation.

Spuren.Bildung²¹, Lea Lugaric, Book 2014, Original Edition of Brockhaus “Bildung. Wissen im 21. Jahrhundert”, 576 pages, Photo: Lea Lugaric

Spuren.Bildung²¹

Lea Lugaric

The current discourse on the future of books and the popularity of electronic texts was the starting point of “Spuren.Bildung²¹”. The artist here engages with the end of printed cyclopaedia Brockhaus in 2014 which was preceded by the triumph of Wikipedia. An original copy of “Brockhaus – Education in the 21st Century” was appropriated and altered to the book object “Spuren.Bildung²¹”. The printing ink was erased by hand thereby making both text and image disappear. Readers are left with searching for traces. The past is becoming readable, an absence visible and a new beginning possible.

Nachtschicht – Ein Tag wie Eis, Nina Mahringer, 2015, photography, Photo: Nina Mahringer

Nachtschicht – Ein Tag wie Eis

Nina Mahringer

The cool beauty in the far north: Helsinki’s peninsula Hanasaari. The first view shows huge industrial buildings and wide, barren plains. This may seem bleak, but the landscape holds its own charm. The strange blue light seems to sink in melancholy, yet it holds an absolute peace embedded in the infinity of white plains. Rosa

Natalie Paloma Maierhofer

With “Rosa” Natalie Maierhofer tries to catch the cool atmosphere of the wintry Helsinki, accompanied by the colours blue and grey. In her work she detects places and buildings that are characterized by differing colours and moods, regardless of day and night.

Chill out, Jianan Qu, 2014, installation, ventilators, 5 m x 30 cm, Photo: Josef Fink

Chill out

Qu Jianan

“Chill out” consists of a series of cooling fans occupying a room. Still in their original packaging they are in full swing, but cannot comply their original purpose – cooling. Quite the contrary, they heat up and produce a deep penetrating humming. From time to time a clock brings them to stop and cool down, thereby putting the room in silence – a moment to chill out.

whiter than sky – B: white, Jianan Qu, Fotografie 2015, Inkjet Paper, Foto: Qu Jianan

whiter than sky – B: white

Qu Jianan

For „Whiter than sky“ Jianan Qu used vintage photographs taken in various Chinese provinces during Mao’s Communist reign from the 1950s up to the 1970s. He digitally removed everything but the background skies in the photographs and left it to the bare silhouettes to suggest the photographs subject: His late grandfather, whose absence can be deeply felt, as his recurring ghostly outlines shine out amongst the greys.

Auf der Suche nach einer frischen Kuhhaut, Franziska Schink, 2014, performative installation, preserved cowhide with salt, europallet, Photo: Franziska Schink, Stepana Cihlova

Auf der Suche nach einer frischen Kuhhaut

Franziska Schink

How does a fresh cowhide actually look from the inside? This question lead the artist to a company for slaughterhouse waste. Once a week she visited the company to look after a sample cowhide over a period of three months. That is when she saw big boxes of animal remains and off cut from meat. It was just a small company, but she learned a lot about animal waste. Her under salt preserved and dried cowhide now lies in between these boxes and containers with bloody animal skins, bones, skulls and meat packaging. In a snow-white salt dress the relict lies on a pallet on the floor. Same as the tanning of cowhide, the process of drying is no longer performed this way in Austria but relocated abroad where environmental standards are lower and labour is cheaper.

KatharinaViktoria, Viktoria Schmid, 2012, installation, video loop on screen, 240 16 mm portraits, Photo: Viktoria Schmid

KatharinaViktoria

Victoria Schmid

“KatharinaViktoria” is commenting on the similarity of two sisters based on 240 looped 16mm single images. Using the inertia of adjustment of the eye, the individual portraits which are being interchanged at very short intervals, become a mixed portrait of the two faces. Thereby emerges a hybrid between photography and film showing this strong connection of the two technologies. In addition to the video projection a mural with 240 stills has been presented, which discloses the structure and technique of the videos.

Lost in Hotel, Heike Schnotale, Daniela Poschauko, Julia Romana Potocnik, 2015, series of photographs, Photo: Heike Schnotale, Daniela Poschauko, Julia Romana Potocnik

Lost In Hotel

Daniela Poschauko, Julia Romana Potocnik, Heike Schnotale

In her series of photographs, the three artists tell the story of “Lost In Hotel”: Lost. I do not recognize anything. Obviously. My throat feels clouded. As if it was blocking every sound that I am trying to make. My eyes wander though the room with paranoia. What time is it? Is someone at the door? I open the door and stride ahead aimlessly. The hotel corridors seem endless. Where should I go? Where does my mind lead me to? Now she is standing in front of me. I am staring at her pale face and can’t find the right words. I can not stand this silence much longer. We sit down. Still no word. What is she thinking? So many question are filling our heads, we are looking at each other, confused and arbitrary. We walk side by side. The night reflects our silence. Into the unknown. And she says goodbye. Alone again.

The photo series “ONLY FOR” is documenting a collection of gender-specific toys. Mapping Design, Doris Schuhmann, 2015, spatial objects, white cardboard (lasercut), Photo: Doris Schuhmann

MAPPING DESIGN - eine Entwurfsmethode

Doris Schuhmann

“Mapping Design” is a design methodology for people in a spatial context. It shows new strategies and links design with science. A cube is divided into shells for the human body – these positions can be taken by clothing, buildings, one-man-rooms or spaces for many people. These forms are potential spaces, some are houses that can be inhabited. They are interconnected and have a neighbourhood. They are part of a bigger picture, yet stand alone. Seen from afar they form a landscape, similar to urban space. In the course of time they change their location, merge or dissociate. These forms are utopian places reflecting a process and stand for the future.

Rampenlicht, Thomas Schwarz, 2014, interaktive installation, ultrasonic sensor, led stripes, Arduino, fingerboard miniramp, 33 x 58 x 50 cm, teaser 1:1:15 – teaser 2:2:15, Photo: Thomas Schwarz

Rampenlicht

Thomas Schwarz

“Rampenlicht” is a modified fingerboard ramp that follows the fingerboarder with a spot light. The position and traveling speed of the fingerboard is calculated by five ultrasonic sensors, which are attached to the side of the “mini ramp”. Using programmable LED strips different illumantion modes can be triggered by pressing a button. The lamp thereby illuminates five seperate areas and generates a fluent light using a dimmer.

SYNKAL 366, Julia Singer, 2015, calendar, book / paper, 13 x 20 cm, Photo: Julia Singer

SYNKAL 366

Julia Singer

“SYNKAL 366” refers to a synaesthesia calendar which can be used as a daily calendar, notebook or diary. SYN stands for synaesthesia, KAL for calendar and 366 for the leap year 2016. Its 366 single colour pages make it possible to dive into the world of people who suffer from synaesthesia for a year: The gradients of the pages are not only based on the individual perception of the synesthete who conducted the project but also on our solar system.

Me is an empty stage

The collection “me is an empty stage” is inspired by both literary and visual discoveries, pursuing the analysis of identity constructions. “Horizons” fathoms both the processes of inscribing and production methods of photography. where is the pipe?, Andreas Tanzer, Daniel Franz Rappitsch, Oliver Naimer, Jonas Fliedl, 2014, mural painting, dispersion paint and tinting, spray cans, 25 x 13 m, Photo: Georg Ritter

where is the pipe? Wandmalerei

Daniel Franz Rappitsch, Jonas Fliedl, Oliver Naimer, Andreas Tanzer

“Where is the pipe?” was developed upon an invitation of the Austrian Cultural attaché in Moscow to design the facade of an apartment block in Magnitogorsk/Russia. The metropolis is split in half by the boundary between Europa and Asia, whereas the industrial area of the city is entirely located on the Asian continent. Hence the life quality differs between the East and the West of the city. The mural makes a statement to connect both continents and suggests that talking to each other helps restore a mutual understanding.

Data Distortion Drawing Machine, Stefan Tiefengraber, 2013/14, installation, drawing machine, mixed media, Photo: Stefan Tiefengraber

Data Distortion Drawing Machine

Stefan Tiefengraber

The “Data Distortion Drawing Machine” is a machine to visualise data in a reverse-drawing process. It draws by grinding of previously applied pastel colours from its surface and thereby creates a distorted data visualisation in about 15 to 20 hours. The distortion of the data is affected by the structure of the paper, the wall on which the machine and the paper are fixed, as well as the machine itself. The process is unique and can not be repeated.

Pigment Lila, Violeta Ivanova, 2015, video series, Photo: Violeta Ivanova

Pigment Lila

Violeta Ivanova

The video series “Pigment Violet” is focused on the movement of small particles. Behind the spectacular depiction of particles of pigment is the physical phenomenon of the Brownian motion, investigating the random motion of particles suspended in a fluid that proves the existence of molecules and atoms. The pigment violet pursues this abstract, cosmic game of the particles – from the logical behaviour at the beginning to utter chaos at the end.

The clouds are not like either one — they do not keep one form forever, Viktoria Schmid, Installation 2014, 3 x 16mm-Projektoren, stumm, S/W mit Farbfilter

The clouds

Viktoria Schmid

With “The Clouds” Viktoria Schmid transfers the process of additive colour mixing (RGB) using a 16 mm film into a spatial installation. The images show an arrangement of a cloudy sky. The structure of the composed image is made visible on three layers and results in a projection, that clearly differs from a conventional picture of a blue sky with white clouds. The same material is shown simultaneously one after another, however, each one has a colour filter altering the black and white footage and giving it a green, blue and red colouration thereby slightly deferring the images. The films are projected as loops through the corresponding colour filter and shown at different stages of overlap. (Text: Julia Dossi)
